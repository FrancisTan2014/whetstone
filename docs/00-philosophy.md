# Philosophy

## Why this exists

Knowledge that isn't recalled decays. Reading without revisiting produces the illusion of learning. Most "learn X every day" apps fail because they grow into guilt-lists — the backlog of overdue cards becomes a wall, the user abandons, the wall stays.

whetstone is built on a different bet: **the user already has a working pattern.** Ten minutes per day of reading 《笠翁对韵》, 《滕王阁序》, and 《洛神赋》 has made those texts stick without forced memorization. That pattern — short, daily, joyful, no pressure — is the spec.

## The four steps

1. **Read** — new input, scoped by daily time budget per subject.
2. **Note** — capture in markdown, plus a one-sentence "what it is."
3. **Speak** — explain it back as if teaching a friend (Feynman). Written or voice. The artifact is checkable.
4. **Connect** — link today's note to at least one existing note.

The next day, recall surfaces yesterday's note before any new input. Each subsequent recall is scheduled by spaced repetition.

## The discipline rule

The daily routine has a **hard cap** on recall items (default: 15). If the SRS queue overflows, the most-overdue and lowest-ease items surface first; the rest are deferred by one day. **The queue never becomes a guilt-list.**

## What "growth through daily discipline" means here

- Discipline is in the *act of returning*, not the volume done.
- Joy is fuel, not a luxury. 《笠翁对韵》's daily slot is a ritual, not a task — it never enters the SRS queue and is never skipped.
- Forgetting is data, not failure. A forgotten card means SRS works; the algorithm will resurface it sooner.

## What this is not

- Not a flashcard app. Notes are first-class; cards are derived from notes.
- Not a productivity tracker. No streaks, no stats, no gamification. The feedback is the routine itself.
- Not a knowledge graph. Linking is one-direction, manual, intentional — not auto-generated.
